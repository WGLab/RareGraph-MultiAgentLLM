A) INCONGRUOUS FEATURES
   The patient may have phenotypes that don't fit the dominant clinical picture
   (e.g., mostly neurodevelopmental but ALSO a skeletal feature).
   Which candidates uniquely explain this unusual combination?

B) TEMPORAL PATTERN
   Does the onset sequence / disease course strongly favor certain candidates?
   (e.g., seizures at 6 months + regression at 18 months)

C) DEMOGRAPHIC ALIGNMENT
   Does sex / age / ethnicity strongly favor or disfavor specific candidates?
   (e.g., Rett syndrome is almost exclusively female; Duchenne is almost exclusively male)

D) GENE-PHENOTYPE COHERENCE
   If a gene/variant is reported, does it point to specific candidates even if
   the phenotypes alone don't obviously converge there?

E) CRITICAL ABSENCES
   Do any highly-ranked candidates have hallmark features the patient
   EXPLICITLY LACKS? These should be demoted. However, if the phenotype is not provided, do not treat it as immediate absence (unless it stated negated/absent from the input).
   Sometimes, this phenotype may not be recorded.
