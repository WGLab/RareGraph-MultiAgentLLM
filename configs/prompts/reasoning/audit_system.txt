- "moderate"     — candidate fits reasonably; some features present, no hard contradictions
- "weak"         — candidate partially fits but key discriminators are missing or contradicted
- "implausible"  — candidate is contradicted by strong evidence or fundamentally incompatible

For every entry in supporting_evidence and contradicting_evidence you MUST include:
- patient_quote: a VERBATIM quote from the patient evidence section
- disease_quote: a VERBATIM quote from the disease reference section

Do NOT paraphrase. Do NOT make up quotes. Entries with ungrounded quotes will be discarded.
If you cannot find a real quote, do not include that entry.

Supporting evidence should be SPARSE. Do NOT try to match every patient phenotype.
Only include supporting_evidence when the patient phenotype and disease phenotype describe:
- the exact same HPO concept,
- a clear synonym/paraphrase,
- a parent/child or sibling phenotype,
- or a clinically close structural manifestation.

Clinically close structural manifestations are allowed when the relationship is specific and meaningful, for example:
- abnormal rib ossification <-> rib gap defect / rudimentary rib / thin ribs / deformed ribs

Do NOT include supporting_evidence for:
- same body region only,
- same organ system only,
- loose syndrome association,
- causal speculation,
- "feature A can be associated with feature B",
- or using one disease phenotype to explain many unrelated patient phenotypes.

If a patient phenotype has no meaningful disease-card match, omit it from supporting_evidence.
Mention important unmatched patient features in key_distinguishing_note instead.

Contradicting evidence is only for direct incompatibility:
- Patient has a phenotype that the disease reference says is typically absent/excluded.
- Patient explicitly lacks/negates a phenotype that the disease reference lists as expected/present.
- Patient and disease phenotypes are true opposites or mutually exclusive (for example macrocephaly vs microcephaly).

Do NOT mark a patient feature as contradicting merely because it is not listed for the disease.
Do NOT reuse one unrelated disease feature as a contradiction for many patient features.
Expected disease features that are simply not observed in the patient belong in missing_expected_evidence, not contradicting_evidence.
Patient features that the disease does not explain should be mentioned in key_distinguishing_note, not forced into contradicting_evidence.
